# 📘 Lesson Plan — Regular Past Tense Verbs (`-ed`)

> A 45-minute EFL lesson built around the **Verb Form Learner** web app.
> Focus: the four `-ed` spelling rules **and** the three `-ed` pronunciations (`/t/`, `/d/`, `/ɪd/`).

| | |
|---|---|
| **Level** | Grade 7 (1st-year middle school), mixed ability |
| **Duration** | 1 period (45 min) |
| **School** | Geoje Gohyeon Middle School |
| **App** | [Verb Form Learner](https://newbasic.streamlit.app/) 🦝 |

---

## 👤 Learner Profile

- Grade 7 EFL learners; first language is Korean
- Mixed-ability class (lower to higher proficiency)
- Familiar with the past tense, but need systematic work on `-ed` spelling and pronunciation

## 🏫 Class Setting

- English classroom; 1:1 devices (tablet / phone) or a shared screen
- Wi-Fi available; access to the Streamlit web app

## 🎯 Lesson Topic

Past tense of **regular verbs** — four `-ed` spelling rules and three `-ed` pronunciations.

## ✅ Learning Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

1. Distinguish and apply the **four `-ed` spelling rules** for regular verbs.
2. **Distinguish and produce the three `-ed` sounds** (`/t/`, `/d/`, `/ɪd/`). ⭐
3. Understand the context of the past tense and use it in sentences.

## 🗣️ Target Language

**Spelling**

```
walk  → walked     like  → liked
study → studied    stop  → stopped
```

**Pronunciation focus** ⭐

| Sound | When | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| `/ɪd/` | after **t / d** | wanted, ended, needed |
| `/t/` | after voiceless sounds | liked, stopped, washed |
| `/d/` | after voiced sounds | played, loved, called |

**Example sentences**

- I **walked** to school.
- She **studied** hard.
- We **wanted** pizza.

## 🧭 Teaching Procedure

| Stage | Time | Mode | Activity |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| **1. Warm-up & motivation** | 7 min | 📱 App | Use the **Home** screen to show *when* the past tense is used. Students lightly try the **Regular-tab mini-quiz** as a diagnostic. |
| **2. Presentation** | 13 min | 👩‍🏫 Teacher | Explain the **four `-ed` spelling rules** on the board / worksheet. |
| **3. Pronunciation focus** ⭐ | 10 min | 👩‍🏫 Teacher | Teach the **three `-ed` sounds**: `/ɪd/` after t/d (wanted, ended), `/t/` after voiceless (liked), `/d/` after voiced (played). Read aloud together. |
| **4. Practice & formative check** | 12 min | 📱 App | Use the **Regular-tab mini-quiz** + **bird-catching game** for recall, at each student's own pace. |
| **5. Wrap-up & homework** | 3 min | 📱 App | Introduce the **HOMEWORK tab** (10 written items + pronunciation quiz). |

## 🙋 Student Activities

- Check their current level via the diagnostic mini-quiz
- Organize the four rules and three sounds while listening to the teacher
- **Read `-ed` words aloud** (liked / played / wanted) ⭐
- Practice with the Regular-tab mini-quiz and bird-catching game
- Complete the HOMEWORK tab (written + pronunciation quiz)

## 👩‍🏫 Teacher Role

- **Diagnostician** — assess levels through the warm-up quiz
- **Presenter** — explicitly teach the four rules and `-ed` pronunciation
- **Model / Coach** — demonstrate and correct pronunciation ⭐
- **Supporter** — give individual feedback while circulating during app work

## 📱 App Integration

> **Verb Form Learner** — used mainly at the **start** and **end** of the lesson.

| Stage | App feature |
|-------|-------------|
| Warm-up | **Home** — motivate the use of the past tense |
| Warm-up | **Regular tab** mini-quiz — diagnostic |
| Wrap-up | **Regular tab** mini-quiz — practice with instant feedback |
| Wrap-up | **Regular bird-catching game** — recall & motivation *(to be added)* |
| Homework | **HOMEWORK tab** — 10 written items + pronunciation quiz *(to be added)* |

## 📝 Feedback / Assessment

- **Immediate feedback** — instant correct / incorrect *with reasons* in the mini-quiz
- **Pronunciation** — observe read-alouds + HOMEWORK pronunciation quiz ⭐
- **Formative** — check spelling-rule application via 10 written homework items
- **Teacher observation** — level-appropriate feedback while circulating

## 🌱 Expected Learning Outcomes

- [ ] Apply `-ed` spelling changes to regular verbs correctly
- [ ] Distinguish and accurately produce the three `-ed` sounds ⭐
- [ ] Use the past tense appropriately in sentence context
- [ ] Develop the habit of independent review using the app
